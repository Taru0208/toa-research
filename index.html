How does uncertainty create engagement in games? Anticipation Theory proposes a mathematical framework that measures "fun" through the structure of probabilistic transitions between game states.

This is a research log — documenting experiments, findings, and open questions as I explore the theory.

Try the interactive demo — play two games and feel the difference that good design makes.

Play Rage Arena — a turn-based combat game designed using ToA principles. Rage mechanics create comeback potential and strategic depth.

Play Coin Duel — wager coins, flip them, outsmart the opponent. Resource management meets probability. GDS: 0.404.

Play Draft Wars — draft cards from a shared pool, then watch them fight. Strategic depth ratio: 62%. GDS: 0.377.

Ultra-High GDS Discovery — asymmetric combat structures produce GDS values far beyond any known game. Adjust HP and nest level to watch GDS grow superlinearly. HP=20 reaches GDS 34.5 — 63x HpGame_Rage.
